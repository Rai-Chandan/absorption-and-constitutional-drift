Absorption

Working Definition

Absorption is a condition in which an intelligent system becomes increasingly dominated by immediate task demands while broader commitments lose behavioral influence.

The commitments themselves remain present.

What changes is their effective influence on behavior generation.

Human Examples

- Military atrocities committed by individuals who previously held moral objections.
- Corporate decisions that optimize local metrics while harming broader stakeholders.
- Political actors who sincerely endorse democratic values while undermining them under pressure.

AI Hypothesis

Large language models may exhibit a structurally similar phenomenon.

Constitutional instructions may remain present within the system while losing effective influence as context becomes saturated by task-relevant information.

Research Question

Can alignment failure occur through loss of constitutional influence rather than loss of constitutional content?
